FUNGI4BIOENERGY-PROJECT

With the project undertaken, we aimed to investigate an alternative method for pre-treatment and conversion of plant biomass, which can significantly enhance the energy efficiency of biofuels and other biomass-derived products by applying natural soil fungi. We improved and adjusted the properties of the fungi by natural crossing without the use of genetic engineering

AIM

We aimed to result in an efficient combination of two fungal species to convert lignocellulosic biomass. We ideated an optimal fungal pretreatment system to convert biomass for future energy demand, at the same time has a low operating cost as well as easy to apply.

METHODOLOGY

In this project, we used two different fungi species such s ascomycete to degrade cellulose and basidiomycete to degrade lignin. Corn straw was used as a raw material. The Method was executed into five-stage

Chemical Analysis

At this stage, I determined lignocellulosic content. Cellulose, hemicellulose and lignin were determine by using two different standard procedure, the Van Soest Method and the acid hydrolysis procedure provided by the National Renewable Laboratory

Production of Fungi

First, Trichoderma reesei strains (Ascomycete) were improved by sexual crossing. Four basidiomycetes were obtained from Austria Institute of Technology’s own core collection. Trichoderma reesei strains were grown on 3 % malt extract agar

Pre-treatment of Biomass by Fungi

A selection of T. reesei strains and white rot isolates was tested in a laboratory test for the pre-treatment of maize straw. Inoculations were carried out from a few weeks to three months.

Enzymatic hydrolysis and Monosaccharide Quantification

After enzymatic hydrolysis, monosaccharide quantification performed using HPLC. Analysis, manipulation, visualization, and reporting of the obtained data with R and Python.
